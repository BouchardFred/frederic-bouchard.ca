About

I am an experimental physicist working in the field of quantum photonics.

Biography

I am a scientist at the National Research Council Canada, where I work on experimental quantum photonics. My research explores how ultrafast optical pulses and nonlinear interactions can be used to generate, control, and measure quantum states of light.

I am particularly interested in temporal-mode encoding, telecom-band quantum optics, and scalable photonic architectures for quantum information processing and communication.

Background

Before joining the National Research Council Canada, I completed my Ph.D. in Physics at the University of Ottawa through the Max Planck Centre for Extreme and Quantum Photonics.

I previously worked on structured light, high-dimensional quantum states, and quantum communication with orbital-angular-momentum modes.
