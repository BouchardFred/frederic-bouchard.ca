About

My work sits at the intersection of ultrafast optics, nonlinear photonics, and quantum information science.

I develop experimental photonic platforms for quantum information processing and quantum communication, with an emphasis on telecom wavelengths, temporal-mode encoding, and nonlinear optical control. Recent work includes programmable ultrafast time-bin circuits, quantum communication with picosecond-scale time bins, and nonlinear optical techniques for manipulating quantum states of light.

Before joining the National Research Council Canada, I completed my Ph.D. in Physics at the University of Ottawa through the Max Planck Centre for Extreme and Quantum Photonics.

Research themes

A few recurring themes define my research program.

Ultrafast quantum photonics

Quantum information encoded in short optical pulses and picosecond-scale temporal modes.

Nonlinear optics

Frequency conversion, Kerr interactions, squeezing, and photon-number-state manipulation.

Quantum communication

Telecom-compatible quantum states and protocols for high-rate photonic quantum technologies.
