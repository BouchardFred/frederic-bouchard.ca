Research

I study quantum photonic systems that use ultrafast optical pulses, temporal modes, and nonlinear optical interactions to process and measure quantum information.

Ultrafast time-bin encoding

Temporal modes provide a natural route toward compact, phase-stable, and high-rate photonic quantum information processing. This work uses picosecond-scale time bins and programmable temporal interferometry to manipulate quantum states of light.

Related publications
  • Programmable Photonic Quantum Circuits with Ultrafast Time-bin Encoding, Physical Review Letters (2024).
  • Photonic quantum walk with ultrafast time-bin encoding, Optica (2024).
  • Measuring ultrafast time-bin qudits, Physical Review A (2023).
  • Quantum communications with ultrafast time-bin qubits, PRX Quantum (2022).

Nonlinear quantum optics at telecom wavelengths

Nonlinear optical interactions can reshape, translate, switch, and measure quantum states of light. I am particularly interested in telecom-compatible implementations using χ² and χ³ processes.

Related publications
  • Efficient quantum frequency translation of broadband single photons by Bragg-scattering four-wave mixing, Optica Quantum (2025).
  • Time-of-flight spectroscopy with ultrafast all-optical gating, Optics Express (2025).
  • Perspectives on all-optical Kerr switching for quantum optical applications, Applied Physics Letters (2021).

Quantum communication

I have worked on quantum communication protocols using both ultrafast temporal encoding and high-dimensional structured photons, with an emphasis on realistic optical channels and noise tolerance.

Related publications
  • Quantum communications with ultrafast time-bin qubits, PRX Quantum (2022).
  • Achieving ultimate noise tolerance in quantum communication, Physical Review Applied (2021).
  • Quantum cryptography with twisted photons through an outdoor underwater channel, Optics Express (2018).
  • High-dimensional intra-city quantum cryptography with structured photons, Optica (2017).

Structured light and high-dimensional quantum optics

Earlier work focused on structured photons, optical angular momentum, high-dimensional quantum states, and their use in quantum cryptography, metrology, and fundamental optics.

Related publications
  • High-dimensional quantum cloning and applications to quantum hacking, Science Advances (2017).
  • Observation of sub-luminal twisted light in vacuum, Optica (2016).
  • Achromatic orbital angular momentum generator, New Journal of Physics (2014).
